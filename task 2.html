Tribute to [Lata Mangeshkar]

Early Life and Career

Lata was born on September 28, 1929, to classical singer and theatre artist Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar and Shevanti in Indore, Madhya Pradesh. Her father started teaching her music at an early age. By the time she was five, Lata was seen participating as an actress in plays written by her father.

Key Accomplishments

  • She lent her voice to over 5,000 songs in over a thousand Hindi and 36 regional films.
  • She had an unprecedented impact on Indian film music.
  • Since 1942, Lata pushed back the boundaries of music with her mind-boggling skills.
  • Mangeshkar was awarded the Legion of Honour, France's highest civilian award, in 2007. The French Ambassador to India said that the award recognized her incomparable singing career and her status as a top artist for over 60 years.

Legacy and Impact

In 2001, Mangeshkar was awarded the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honour. In the same year, she established the Master Deenanath Mangeshkar Hospital in Pune, managed by the Lata Mangeshkar Medical Foundation (founded by the Mangeshkar family in October 1989)..
